The Friendster Story — Context

Friendster launched in 2002 as one of the world's first social networks, reaching 115 million users before Facebook existed. Kent was there from the start — early advisor, then first management hire, eventually running the company as CEO. The experience of watching a pioneering network-effect business rise and struggle (eventually pivoting to gaming in Asia) gives Kent a rare vantage point on what makes network effects work and what kills them.

His co-founder Jonathan Abrams founded Friendster. The fact that they're still building together 20+ years later — through Friendster, Nuzzel, and now 8-Bit — says something about the partnership's durability.

What They're Building

OSARO develops hardware-agnostic AI software for industrial robots. Their core platform, SightWorks™, is a perception engine that lets robots make real-time decisions for high-variability warehouse tasks.⁴

  • Robotic Picking: High-SKU piece picking for e-commerce fulfillment — handles fragile items, complex packaging
  • Robotic Bagging: Variable SKU and product shape handling for pack-and-ship workflows
  • Robotic Kitting: Precision assembly of variable components and product geometries
  • Robotic Depalletization: Mixed-case depal with variable packaging, sizes, orientations
  • AutoModel™: Learns on the fly — handles new SKUs without interruption or reprogramming

Technical Differentiation

  • Software-first: Hardware-agnostic — integrates with ASRS, AMRs, conveyors, any robot arm
  • Perception + Control: Combines ML-based object recognition with adaptive control software
  • Cloud GPU infrastructure: Data pipeline and training at scale
  • VLA Foundation Model (2025): Partnered with NVIDIA's GR00T to build vision-language-action model using behavioral cloning and production data⁴
  • No 3D sensors needed: Trains from 2D video demonstrations — adapts to lighting, occlusion, object variation

The Space — Warehouse Automation

The warehouse automation market is driven by persistent labor shortages, e-commerce growth, and rising consumer expectations for speed. Key dynamics:

  • Labor crisis: Warehouse worker turnover exceeds 100% annually; automation is no longer optional for large fulfillment operators
  • Mixed-SKU problem: The hardest unsolved piece — robots that can pick arbitrary items from bins without pre-programming each SKU
  • Humanoid hype vs. reality: Derik is publicly skeptical of humanoids in logistics — calls out battery life, payload, safety, and cost barriers. Believes purpose-built systems win.⁷
  • AI models maturing: LLM/transformer-augmented perception is entering production, but systems still need domain-specific data and model adaptation

OSARO's differentiation is being software-first and hardware-agnostic — they don't sell robots, they sell the AI brain that makes existing robots intelligent. This gives them flexibility to integrate into diverse customer environments.

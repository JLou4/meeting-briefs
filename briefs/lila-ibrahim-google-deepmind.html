Who She Is — The 30-Second Version

Lila Ibrahim is Google DeepMind's first-ever Chief AI Readiness Officer — a new role she moved into in January 2026 after serving as DeepMind's founding COO for nearly 8 years (2018–2026). Her mandate: prepare the world for increasingly powerful AI. She leads Frontier AI Global Affairs, Strategic Initiatives, a Public Engagement Lab, DeepMind's Impact Accelerator, and the Responsibility team.

Before DeepMind, she was COO/President of Coursera (2013–2017), Senior Operating Partner at Kleiner Perkins, and spent 18 years at Intel — including Chief of Staff to CEO Craig Barrett and running emerging markets from Shanghai and Hong Kong.

She's not a researcher — she's an operator and bridge-builder. Her career pattern is: take bleeding-edge tech and operationalize it for real-world impact. At Intel it was chips in emerging markets. At Coursera it was scaling MOOCs. At DeepMind it was turning a research lab into a responsible, production-grade AI organization. Now she's the person Google trusts to shape how frontier AI meets the real world.

What She Actually Does at DeepMind

Her current role is essentially DeepMind's envoy to the world. Specifics:

  • Frontier AI Global Affairs — policy, government engagement, regulatory strategy
  • Strategic Initiatives & Public Engagement Lab — partnerships, public communication about AI progress
  • Impact Accelerator — deploying AI for social good (health, climate, education)
  • Responsibility Team — embedding safety and ethics into research from day one (not bolted on after)
Her guiding question: "How do we make sure AI is happening with us and not to us?" This isn't marketing — she helped DeepMind build a framework where responsibility is parallel to research, not a compliance checkbox. She co-signed the Center for AI Safety extinction-risk statement alongside Demis Hassabis and Shane Legg.
